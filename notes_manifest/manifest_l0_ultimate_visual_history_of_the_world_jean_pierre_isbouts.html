"... Another way of imagining the story of our planet is to compress the whole 4.6 billion years of Earth history into 24 hours -- a single day, starting from midnight.

There are several ways to do that, but according to one scenario, the first multicell organisms appear shortly after 6 a.m. .

Then we have to wait a very long time, some 16 hours until 10 p.m. at night, for plants and animal life to emerge.

At 10:45p.m., the dinosaurs make their appearance, followed 45 minutes later by mammals.

With just 20 minutes to go before the end of our Earth day, the dinosaurs are wiped out by a cataclysmic event (such as abrupt climate change as a result of an asteroid impact).

It's only just before midnight, at a minute to 12, that Homo sapiens begins hunting and gathering..."
